Set Operations

\[ \newcounter{section} \newcounter{subsection} \counterwithin{subsection}{section} \] \[\stepcounter{section}\]

\(\thesection\) Union

The union (denoted by \(\cup\)) of a collection of sets is the set of all elements in the collection.

The union of two sets \(A\) and \(B\) is \[ \begin{equation} A \cup B = \{ x \mid x \in A \text{ or } x \in B \} \,. \end{equation} \]

\[\stepcounter{subsection}\]

\(\thesubsection\) Notation

For a union of sets \(S_{1}, S_{2}, S_{3}, \dots, S_{n}\) one can use the notation \(\bigcup_{i=1}^{n} S_{i}\) instead of \(S_{1} \cup S_{2} \cup S_{3} \cup \dots \cup S_{n}\).

\[\stepcounter{subsection}\]

\(\thesubsection\) Properties

The union of sets is commutative, associative, and idempotent. That is, for any sets \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\): \[ \begin{align} A \cup B &= B \cup A \\ A \cup (B \cup C) &= (A \cup B) \cup C \\ A \cup A &= A \end{align} \]

\[\stepcounter{section}\]

\(\thesection\) Intersection

The intersection (denoted by \(\cap\)) of a collection of sets is the set containing every element \(x\) where \(x\) belongs to every set in the collection.

The intersection of two sets \(A\) and \(B\) is \[ \begin{equation} A \cap B = \{ x \mid x \in A \text{ and } x \in B \} \,. \end{equation} \]

\[\stepcounter{subsection}\]

\(\thesubsection\) Notation

For an intersection of sets \(S_{1}, S_{2}, S_{3}, \dots, S_{n}\) one can use the notation \(\bigcap_{i=1}^{n} S_{i}\) instead of \(S_{1} \cap S_{2} \cap S_{3} \cap \dots \cap S_{n}\).

\[\stepcounter{subsection}\]

\(\thesubsection\) Properties

The intersection of sets is commutative, associative, and idempotent. That is, for any sets \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\): \[ \begin{align} A \cap B &= B \cap A \\ A \cap (B \cap C) &= (A \cap B) \cap C \\ A \cap A &= A \end{align} \]
